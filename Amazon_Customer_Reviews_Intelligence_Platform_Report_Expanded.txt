Amazon Customer Reviews Intelligence Platform

Final Project Report

1. Project Objective

Develop an end-to-end Natural Language Processing (NLP) pipeline capable
of automatically classifying customer sentiment, grouping Amazon
products into meaningful categories, and generating AI-assisted buying
guides from customer reviews. The resulting models were later integrated
into a separate Streamlit deployment.

2. Introduction

Customer reviews provide valuable insights into product quality and
customer satisfaction. This project develops an end-to-end NLP pipeline
to automatically analyze Amazon reviews using sentiment classification,
product clustering, and generative AI.

3. Business Problem

The objective is to transform thousands of customer reviews into
actionable insights by automatically identifying customer sentiment,
organizing products into meaningful categories, and generating buying
recommendations.

4. Dataset

Source: Amazon Product Reviews (Kaggle)

-   Original dataset: 34,660 reviews
-   Final cleaned dataset: 34,626 reviews
-   21 original variables
-   Reviews from Amazon devices including Fire Tablets, Kindle, Echo,
    Fire TV, and Accessories

Sentiment labels: - Ratings 1–2 → Negative - Rating 3 → Neutral -
Ratings 4–5 → Positive

5. Exploratory Data Analysis

The dataset was explored through missing-value analysis, duplicate
detection, rating distributions, sentiment distributions, review length
analysis, product and brand statistics, and text exploration.

Key findings: - More than 93% of reviews were positive, producing a
highly imbalanced dataset. - Missing values were concentrated in user
metadata and removed during preprocessing. - Product ratings were
strongly skewed toward 5-star reviews. - These observations motivated
the use of balanced classifiers and Macro-F1 as the primary evaluation
metric.

6. Data Cleaning

The preprocessing pipeline removed duplicates, handled missing values,
selected relevant features, merged review titles with review text into a
single input field, and created the final three-class sentiment target.

7. Methodology

Sentiment Classification: - Dummy Classifier - TF-IDF + Logistic
Regression - TF-IDF + Tuned Linear SVM - DistilBERT

DistilBERT achieved the best predictive performance. Linear SVM was
selected for deployment because it provides fast inference, lower
computational requirements, and explainable predictions.

Product Clustering: - TF-IDF - Truncated SVD - K-Means

Products were grouped into four interpretable categories that served as
the foundation for the recommendation engine and generative AI.

Generative AI: Google FLAN-T5 Small generated structured buying guides
containing: - Overview - Top Recommendations - Common Complaints -
Buying Recommendation

8. Model Evaluation

Dummy Classifier Accuracy: 93.3% Macro-F1: 0.322 TF-IDF + Logistic
Regression Accuracy: 89.0% Macro-F1: 0.562 TF-IDF + Linear SVM Accuracy:
93.2% Macro-F1: 0.577 DistilBERT Accuracy: 95.2% Macro-F1: 0.704

Discussion: Logistic Regression established a strong baseline. Linear
SVM improved Macro-F1 while remaining lightweight and interpretable.
DistilBERT achieved the highest predictive performance, especially on
minority classes, but its computational cost made Linear SVM the
preferred model for deployment.

9. Deployment

The research repository was separated from the deployment repository. A
dedicated Streamlit application integrates the trained models into three
modules: - Category Summaries - Sentiment Explorer - Customer Personas

10. Results

-   34,626 cleaned customer reviews
-   Four evaluated sentiment classification models
-   Best-performing model: DistilBERT (95.2% Accuracy, Macro-F1 0.704)
-   Four interpretable product clusters
-   AI-generated buying guides
-   Interactive Streamlit deployment

11. Limitations

The main limitation is the severe class imbalance, particularly for
Neutral and Negative reviews, which affected minority-class performance.

12. Future Work

Potential improvements include: - Class balancing techniques - Larger
transformer models - Multilingual support - Retrieval-Augmented
Generation (RAG) - Live review ingestion through APIs

13. Conclusion

This project demonstrates a complete NLP workflow from raw customer
reviews to actionable business insights by combining supervised
learning, unsupervised learning, generative AI, and interactive
deployment.
